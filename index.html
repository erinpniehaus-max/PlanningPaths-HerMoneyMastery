💡 Why Your Numbers Come First

You cannot build a financial plan on guesswork. Before investing, before retirement planning, before anything else — you need to know exactly where you stand today. Think of this as your financial GPS: you need a starting point before you can navigate to your destination.

🛡️ Your Financial Safety Net

Before investing aggressively, you need three layers of protection: an emergency fund, adequate insurance, and a debt elimination plan. Think of these as the foundation of your financial house — without them, everything you build on top is at risk.

💡 Key Insight: If you have debt at 7%+ interest, paying it off IS an investment — guaranteed returns no stock market can match.

Insurance Essentials for Women 40–60

  • Life Insurance: If anyone depends on your income, you need term life insurance. Simple, affordable, effective.
  • Disability Insurance: Your earning power is your greatest asset. Long-term disability replaces income if you cannot work.
  • Long-Term Care: Women are more likely to need long-term care and for longer periods. Consider purchasing between 55–65.
  • Umbrella Insurance: Extra liability coverage beyond home and auto. Inexpensive for the protection it offers.

📈 The Power of Compound Growth

Investing is how you make your money work for you instead of the other way around. The earlier you start and the more consistently you contribute, the more powerful compounding becomes. A woman who invests $500/month starting at age 40 with an 8% return will have approximately $366,000 by age 60. Start at 45? About $228,000. Every year matters.

Account Priority Order:
1️⃣ Maximize employer 401(k) match (free money!)
2️⃣ Fund Roth IRA or Backdoor Roth
3️⃣ Max out HSA if available
4️⃣ Increase 401(k) to annual limit
5️⃣ Use taxable brokerage for additional investing

Key Investment Products

  • Index Funds / ETFs: Broad market diversification, ultra-low fees. The foundation of most smart portfolios.
  • Target-Date Funds: Auto-adjusts your mix as you approach retirement. "Set it and forget it."
  • Bonds: Stability and income. Cushion against stock volatility.
  • REITs: Real estate exposure without buying property. Good income generators.
  • Dividend Stocks: Companies that share profits with you. Excellent approaching retirement.

📊 Understanding What Markets Tell You

You do not need to predict the market. You need to understand it well enough to stay disciplined when others panic. Since 1928, the S&P 500 has experienced over 25 bear markets — and every single one was followed by a recovery.

🧠 Your Biggest Risk Is Your Own Behavior:
• Loss Aversion — Pain of losing $1,000 feels 2x worse than gaining $1,000
• Recency Bias — Assuming recent trends continue forever
• Herd Mentality — Following the crowd into "hot" investments
• Anchoring — Fixating on your purchase price instead of current value

Key Economic Indicators

  • GDP Growth — Overall economic health. Strong = bullish.
  • CPI (Inflation) — High CPI = rate hike fears.
  • Fed Funds Rate — Rate cuts = bullish; hikes = tightening.
  • Unemployment — Rising unemployment = concern.
  • Yield Curve — Inverted curve has historically signaled recession.

🏖️ Retirement Planning for Women

Women face distinct retirement challenges: longer life expectancy means more years to fund, potential career gaps reduce Social Security benefits, and healthcare costs are statistically higher. A 65-year-old woman should expect to spend $165,000+ on healthcare throughout retirement — not including long-term care.

Social Security Claiming Strategy:
• Claim at 62 → benefit reduced up to 30% permanently
• Full Retirement Age (67) → full benefit
• Delay to 70 → benefit increases 8% per year past FRA
• For women in good health, delaying until 70 can mean tens of thousands more over a lifetime
Tax-Efficient Withdrawal Order:
1️⃣ Taxable accounts first (favorable capital gains rates)
2️⃣ Tax-deferred accounts (Traditional IRA/401k)
3️⃣ Roth accounts last (tax-free growth as long as possible)

🏛️ Protect Everything You've Built

Building wealth is only half the equation. Protecting it through estate planning, tax strategy, and legal documents ensures your money goes where YOU want it to — not where the government or courts decide.

⚠️ Critical: Beneficiary designations on retirement accounts and life insurance override your will. If your ex-spouse is still listed as beneficiary on your 401(k), they will receive the money regardless of what your will says. Review these annually!

Essential Estate Documents

  • Will — Directs asset distribution, names guardians for dependents.
  • Revocable Living Trust — Avoids probate, maintains privacy.
  • Durable Power of Attorney — Names someone for financial decisions if you're incapacitated.
  • Healthcare Directive — Specifies medical wishes.
  • Beneficiary Designations — Review on ALL accounts annually.
Choosing an Advisor:
• Fee-Only — Paid only by you, not commissions
• Fiduciary — Legally required to act in YOUR best interest
• CFP® — Comprehensive education and ethics requirements
• Find one at NAPFA.org
